What is SSL certificate?



What is an SSL Certificate?

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) Certificate is very important especially for information sensitive websites. It is a data encryption layer which provides communications security over a computer network.

If your website is an e-commerce website or you take some kind of sales activity on your website, you should definitely obtain an SSL Certificate from an authorized service provider. SSL encrypts the data that is transmitted between the user's browser and the web server, making it much more difficult for hackers or other malicious actors to intercept and steal sensitive information.

All modern browsers like Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, Google Chrome, and Safari mark your website 'unsafe' if your website does not hold an SSL Certificate. SSL certificate also helps you in SEO and search engine rankings. The most popular search engines such as Google, Bing, Yandex, and Yahoo check if your website have an SSL Certificate to rank your website higher on search results.

Additionally, SSL certificate can also be used to authenticate the website identity, This can be useful for organizations or individuals who want to ensure that their website is recognized as a legitimate and trustworthy source of information.

There are mainly three types of SSL certificate:

- DV (Domain Validation): To verify the ownership of a domain.

- OV (Organization Validation): To verify the authenticity of an organization

- EV (Extended Validation): This is the highest level of authentication. It is validating the organization and its physical and operational existence.

There are five the most popular providers of SSL certificate: Comodo, DigiCert, GlobalSign, GoDaddy, and Namecheap.

What is a Wildcard SSL Certificate?

A Wildcard SSL certificate is an SSL certificate which covers all your subdomains. What is a subdomain? Subdomain is a first part of your domain address. For example, in www.example.com "www" part is a subdomain.

A subdomain can be different than "WWW". You can put "api", "application", "country" or "whatever" as a subdomain. Usually domain providers allow you to create a great deal of subdomains, if not unlimited.

A single Wildcard SSL certificate will cover all of your subdomains. To obtain a Wildcard SSL certificate, just put '*' symbol as a subdomain when you buy an SSL certificate. For instance, if your domain is example.com, enter *.example.com in the domain section when you obtain an SSL certificate.

Just don't forget to put also your root domain when you get your SSL certificate. Because a Wildcard SSL certificate will cover only subdomains, not the root domain. So you need to specify the root domain separately. But www.example.com will be covered among with the other subdomains of your domain name.



Is there a free option for SSL Certificate?

Yes, there is a free SSL certificate provider called Let's Encrypt. You can search on Google and other popular search engines and get idea about Let's Encrypt.

But there are some disadvantages of using Let's Encrypt. The first and the most important disadvantage is you can not issue Let's Encrypt SSL certificate for more than three months.

You also need to install the issued SSL certificate on your server manually. Please also note that you can not issue a wildcard SSL certificate with the help of Let's Encrypt as of today.

But it will not be a concern for you as long as you do not have a URL routing in place other than the www subdomain. Because the www subdomain will already be included in Let’s Encrypt SSL certificate when you obtain it.



The most significant benefits of having an SSL certificate.

- The most important advantage of SSL certificate is data encryption. It is very crucial to have an SSL certificate especially if your users enter their sensitive data to submit forms on your website such as personal information, contact details, address, credit card details etc.

- Another advantage is that SSL certificate protects your website against hackers, cyber criminals, or identity thieves. An extra encryption layer makes it very hard for internet pirates to penetrate into your website or steal sensitive information from it.

- SSL certificate leads to a better SEO ranking. Google, Bing, Yandex and all other popular search engines evaluate the existence of a properly configured SSL certificate.

- SSL certificate confirms that your business is genuine and you care about the safety of your clients. It will give an attractive appearance and create a good impression on your potential clients.

- All the most popular web browsers will show your website as “insecure” if your website lacks an SSL certificate. This situation seems like a bad impression on the users who visit your site the first time. Therefore, having an SSL certificate is a must for the websites who prioritize user experience and trust in these days.
